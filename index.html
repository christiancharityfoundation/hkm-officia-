acting from a rights-based approach

PFC focus is on enabling children to fulfil their fundamental right to education by eliminating the obstacles that prevent them from accessing a full course of quality, primary education.

selecting priority countries

PFC undertakes initial research to identify priority countries with large numbers of out of school children, as well as partners with a demonstrated track record of successful interventions in these countries. PFC works with partners by providing technical and financial support to scaleup existing initiatives.

Relying on solid analytical underpinnings

PFC undertakes analytical work to identify countries with large numbers of OOSC and bases its decisions of where to work on sound educational data and research from internationally recognised sources. The research and data show national, regional and other disparities, indicate domestic and international investments in education, and note major partners that are active in the area, among other factors that contribute to decision making.



working within the context of national education plans

PFC recognizes that education plays a fundamental role in individual, community and national development, and is a national responsibility. Although PFC does not usually fund governments, it does ensure that the activities it supports are consistent with national education plans.

emphasising quality for retention and learning

PFC is based on the principle that every person has the fundamental right to a quality primary education that will contribute to that individual being able to act on his or her behalf and make informed choices that lead to a dignified and meaningful life.

creating added value

PFC recognises the value of different types of partnership and works with partner organisations to support innovative programs and methods of education for the hardest-to-reach children, especially those affected by poverty, conflict, natural disaster and cultural barriers.
